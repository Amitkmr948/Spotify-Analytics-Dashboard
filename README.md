# Spotify Streaming Analytics Dashboard

A Power BI dashboard that analyzes global streaming patterns, artist performance, song popularity, and release trends using Spotify chart data.  
The project uncovers which artists dominate the charts, how popularity changes over time, and what types of releases (albums, singles, or compilations) drive the highest engagement.

---

## Project Overview

This dashboard provides a three-page analytical view:

1. **Overview Page** – Overall popularity, artist distribution, song types, and monthly trends  
2. **Songs Page** – Song-level popularity, hits per artist, and detailed track analytics  
3. **Artists Page** – Artist-specific performance, track volume, and average engagement metrics  

The dataset includes **789 distinct songs**, **342 artists**, with an average popularity score of **90**, making it a strong indicator of listener preference and market demand.

---

## Key Insights

### **1. Artist Performance**
- Taylor Swift dominates with **85+ songs**, far above any other artist.
- Other high-volume creators include:
  - Travis Scott – 30 songs  
  - Drake – 27 songs  
  - Bad Bunny – 22 songs  
- Engagement is heavily concentrated among top global artists.

### **2. Song Popularity**
- Top tracks reach **50K–51K popularity**, showing strong replay value.
- Popularity is skewed toward the top 20–30 songs, confirming a “hits-driven” consumption pattern.

### **3. Album Type Trends**
- **Albums:** 562 releases  
- **Singles:** 269 releases  
- **Singles contribute 36% of total popularity**, outperforming albums on a per-song basis.

### **4. Explicit vs Non-Explicit Content**
- Non-explicit songs account for **higher total popularity** than explicit tracks.
- Broader playlist accessibility increases reach and engagement.

### **5. Release Year & Volume**
- **2023 releases:** 423  
- **2024 releases:** 452  
- Steady growth shows rising content output and competition.

### **6. Monthly Listening Trends**
- Popularity peaks in **Jan, Feb, and May** (around 92 avg).
- Dips occur in **September–October**, before rising again in December.
- Listener behavior aligns with seasonal and promotional cycles.

---

## Business Impact Summary

The analysis highlights:

### **Strengths**
- Strong popularity consistency throughout the year  
- High engagement from major global artists  
- Singles provide high ROI in terms of listener reach  

### **Risks**
- Heavy dependence on top artists  
- Explicit content limits playlist eligibility  
- Seasonal dips may reduce ad revenue and engagement  

---

## Strategic Recommendations

### **1. Playlist Strategy**
- Promote high-performing artists and singles to maximize engagement.
- Build targeted playlists for low-activity months (Sep–Oct).

### **2. Content Acquisition**
- Prioritize artists with proven sustained popularity.
- Secure non-explicit versions of trending songs for broader use.

### **3. Release Campaign Optimization**
- Launch major promotions in months where popularity naturally dips.
- Boost visibility for emerging artists with rising popularity metrics.

### **4. User Engagement**
- Create push notifications and curated playlists around trending tracks.
- Highlight singles in homepage placement due to their strong performance.
